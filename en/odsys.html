Organization Development

From System Thinking perspective

Systems Thinking is a growing discipline designed to tackle complexity and produce significant results as we strive to understand and guide organizational change. It is a structured approach that emphasizes examining problems more completely and accurately in developing and implementing solutions.
Systems Thinking focuses on:
    • Interactions between parts, not the parts themselves
    • The way one system affects other systems
    • Reoccurring patterns rather than just individual events
    • Change over time (Delay)
    • How feedback affects the parts

Systems thinking allows us to more effectively examine complexities, and recognize “leverage points” (the point at which modifications will be most meaningful). With this approach the root cause of issues is uncovered so that the accurate “leverage point” can be identified, addressed and improved, creating positive impacts that reverberate throughout the entire system.

Meaningful change is not top-down or even bottom-up; it is participative at all levels and aligned through a common understanding of the system. Systems Thinking involves both a mindset that focuses on how the parts of a whole are interrelated.

In addition to this mindset, Systems Thinking relies on a variety of specialized tools to graphically depict a system’s structure and behaviour, achieve a collective understanding of the issues, and create a collaborative problem-solving approach so that high impact interventions can be developed.

Organizational issues are examined using Causal Loop Diagrams, System Archetypes, Feedback Loops. By employing the mindset and tools of Systems Thinking, organizations build a shared vision, enhance collaboration, foster a learning environment, and increase idea generation. Groups within the organization learn together, generating knowledge and understanding beyond what any one person already knows.

An important factor in moving towards Systems Thinking is to define an overall vision, mission and goals of the organization. Because Systems Thinking sees the organization as a complete system, it is essential that the strategies and plans of each part of the organization be aligned. Everyone needs to be on the same page. Silo thinking must move towards global thinking, with the organization viewing itself as one team, not a conglomeration of multiple teams. More than a collection of tools and methods, Systems Thinking is an underlying philosophy. It recognizes that the increasing complexity and interdependence of organizational issues creates new challenges that require a paradigm shift in our approach to problem solving, idea generation and decision-making. Ultimately, organizations that utilize Systems Thinking will improve the quality of their decisions and enjoy greater success.
